# Spread 08 — Activity: Build a tiny dataset

> Part 2: How does it work? · spread 8 of 16 · Activity A2 · 15 minutes

## Heading

**Activity 2: Build a tiny dataset.**

## Body

You are going to build a dataset.

It will be a tiny one — five rows. But it will be real, and it will be yours.

## Instructions

> Fill in the table with **5 people from your life**. They can be family, friends, neighbors, classmates — anyone.
>
> Write what *they* would say, not what *you* would guess. If you can, ask them. If you can't, write what you remember them saying.

## Reflection — answer in the spaces below

**1. Who did you leave out?** Who in your life didn't make your top 5? Why?

(blank space for ~3 lines)

**2. If a computer learned about "favorite food" from only your dataset, what kinds of food would it think are popular? What kinds would it never know about?**

(blank space for ~3 lines)

**3. What if your dataset had 1,000 people from your community instead of 5? What answers would change? What would stay the same?**

(blank space for ~3 lines)

## Bonus prompt (margin)

> How many of your 5 people speak Spanish at home?
>
> If a computer learned about your community **only** from your dataset, would it know Spanish was spoken there?

## What this teaches

You just experienced what AI engineers call "dataset construction." Every choice you made — who to include, how to phrase the question, what to leave out — shaped the data. Real AI datasets are built by people making the same kinds of choices, just at a much bigger scale.
