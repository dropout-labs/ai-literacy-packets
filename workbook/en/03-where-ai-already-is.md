# Spread 03 — Where AI already is in your life

> Part 1: What is AI? · spread 3 of 16

## Heading

**It's already here.**

## Opening question (large, top of page)

> Think about the last 24 hours. How many times do you think you used AI — or AI used something about you — without thinking about it?

## Body

Most people guess two or three. The honest answer is closer to fifty.

Here's a partial list. Mark the ones that happened to you in the last day:

- ☐ Your phone unlocked when it saw your face
- ☐ A song or video showed up in your feed because something decided you'd like it
- ☐ A search bar finished your sentence before you finished typing
- ☐ Your phone's keyboard suggested the next word
- ☐ A camera app made a photo look better automatically
- ☐ Your school used software that scored, sorted, or flagged something you wrote
- ☐ A store, ad, or app changed what it showed you based on what it knew about you
- ☐ A voice assistant (Siri, Alexa, Google) understood you when you spoke
- ☐ An email app sorted a message into spam without you asking
- ☐ A maps app picked a route, knowing about traffic it couldn't see from where you were

If you marked four or more, you used AI today.

## The point of this page

You did not sign up for AI. You did not download AI. You did not push a button labeled AI.

It's just there.

The question this packet is going to keep asking — over and over — is **not** *should AI exist?* AI exists. The question is **what do you want to do about that?**

> "AI" stands for **artificial intelligence**. We'll explain what that actually means on the next spread. For now, just notice: it's already in your day.
