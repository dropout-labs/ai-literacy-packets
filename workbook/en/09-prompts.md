# Spread 09 — Prompts: how to ask AI for what you want

> Part 3: How to use it well · spread 9 of 16

## Heading

**Asking is a skill.**

## Opening question

Imagine you walked up to a stranger at a bus stop and said:

> "Tell me about cars."

What would they say back? Probably "...what about cars?"

That's how AI feels when you give it a vague prompt. It doesn't know what you want, so it gives you something generic — and then you blame the AI.

The skill isn't in the AI. **The skill is in the asking.**

## The three things a good prompt tells AI

A useful prompt tells AI three things:

1. **What you want.** A list? A poem? An explanation? A step-by-step? A rewrite of something you already wrote?
2. **What you already know.** So it doesn't waste your time on basics you don't need.
3. **Who it's for.** You? A 6-year-old? A teacher who's strict about citations? Your grandma?

## Worked example

```
BAD                    →   "tell me about cars"

OKAY                   →   "tell me about used cars under $5000"

GOOD                   →   "Make me a list of 5 questions
                            to ask a mechanic when I'm
                            buying a used car for under $5000.
                            I don't know much about engines.
                            The list is for me — I'm 16."
```

The good version got specific about:
- the **format** (a list of 5 questions)
- the **constraint** ($5,000 budget, used)
- the **audience** (the youth themselves, age 16, no engine background)

## Why this matters

Two people can ask AI the same kind of question and get wildly different answers, just based on **how they asked**. The person who asks well gets useful answers. The person who asks vaguely gets generic answers — and then thinks AI is dumb.

It's not. They didn't tell it what they wanted.

## Another worked example

```
BAD     "help with homework"

GOOD    "I have to write a 3-paragraph essay about why
         I think Romeo and Juliet's parents are at fault
         for what happened. I have my main argument
         already (their feud made their kids hide their
         relationship). I need help organizing the
         3 paragraphs. Don't write the essay — give me
         a sentence-by-sentence outline I can fill in."
```

Notice the GOOD version told AI **what not to do** ("Don't write the essay"). That matters too.

## The big idea

> The quality of what AI gives you depends almost entirely on the quality of what you ask. **Practice asking.** The next page is your turn.
