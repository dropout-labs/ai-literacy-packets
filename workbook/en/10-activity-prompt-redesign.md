## Instructions

> A good prompt tells AI three things:
>
> 1. **What you want** (a list? a poem? an explanation? a step-by-step?)
> 2. **What you already know** (so it doesn't waste time on basics)
> 3. **Who it's for** (you? a 6-year-old? a teacher?)
>
> Read each bad prompt below. Rewrite it to include those three things. Use the space provided.

## Reflection at the bottom

Look at your three rewrites.

- **Which one was hardest to rewrite?** Why?
- **Which is more powerful: a person who knows the answer, or a person who knows how to ask the right question?**

## Margin sidebar — worked example

> **Bad:** "tell me about cars"
>
> **Better:** "Make me a list of 5 questions to ask a mechanic when I'm buying a used car for under $5,000. I don't know much about engines. The list is for me — I'm 16."

## What this teaches

You just practiced one of the most valuable skills for working with AI: turning vague wants into clear asks. This skill works on humans too.
