## Body

This packet is yours.

You don't have to give it back. You don't have to finish it. Nobody is grading it. There is no test at the end.

It's about AI — the technology that's already grading your homework, deciding what shows up on your phone, and screening your parents' job applications. We made this because most of what's written about AI is written for people who already have the words for it. You shouldn't have to learn the words just to take care of yourself.

You can do this packet:

- alone, with a pencil, at a kitchen table
- with a friend, taking turns
- with a parent or older sibling, especially the conversation pages near the end
- one page at a time, over a week, or all in one sitting

There are five **activities** in here, where you actually do something with a pencil. There are four big questions the packet keeps coming back to:

1. What is AI, really?
2. How does it actually work?
3. How do I use it well?
4. How do I notice when it's wrong, biased, or dangerous?

The Spanish version of this whole packet is on the right-hand pages, every spread. Read whichever side you want. Both sides say the same thing.

## A note for the adult who picked this up

If you're a parent, grandparent, librarian, or older sibling reading this alongside a young person — welcome. The conversation guide on spread 15 is for you and them together. There's nothing on these pages you need to already know. Your instincts about scams, manipulation, and trust are exactly the instincts AI literacy is built on.
