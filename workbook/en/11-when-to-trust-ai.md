# Spread 11 — When to trust AI, when to double-check

> Part 3: How to use it well · spread 11 of 16

## Heading

**Trust, but check.**

## Body

AI sounds confident. That's a problem.

When AI gives you an answer, it doesn't say "I'm not sure" most of the time, even when it should. It says it like it knows. That confidence can fool you.

The skill is learning **when AI's confidence matches reality, and when it doesn't.**

## A simple rubric

Look at what you're asking AI for. It probably falls into one of these three categories:

### ✅ Usually safe to trust

- Helping you brainstorm ideas
- Rewriting something you wrote to be clearer
- Summarizing a document you can check
- Translating a phrase you can verify
- Explaining a concept you can compare to other sources
- Generating a first draft you'll edit anyway

These are tasks where AI's confidence is roughly trustworthy, and where you can catch problems easily.

### ⚠️ Double-check before using

- Anything with **specific facts**: dates, names, statistics, prices
- Anything with **citations or sources** (AI often invents these)
- Anything **about a real person**, especially someone less famous
- Anything **legal, medical, or financial**
- Anything you're going to **submit somewhere** as your own work

For these: use AI as a starting point, but verify the specifics from a real source.

### 🛑 Do not trust AI alone

- Decisions about your **health**, your **money**, or your **safety**
- **Legal questions** that affect you or your family
- **Anything about another person** (their address, their history, their identity)
- **Things you can't undo** (sending an important email, signing something, telling someone something)
- **Anything that could hurt someone** if it's wrong

For these: AI can help you think. AI cannot decide for you.

## A useful question to ask yourself

> If this answer is wrong, what happens?

If the answer is "nothing, I'll try again" — go ahead, trust AI.

If the answer is "I get the wrong grade / I lose money / I hurt someone / I can't take it back" — verify. Always.

## The big idea

> AI is confident even when it's wrong. **Your job is to know when its confidence has earned your trust.**
