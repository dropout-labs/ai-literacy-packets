# Spread 04 — Patterns: what AI is actually doing

> Part 1: What is AI? · spread 4 of 16

## Heading

**It's mostly about patterns.**

## Opening scenario

Imagine you have a friend who has eaten lunch with you every weekday for a year. After a while, you can guess what they're going to order. Not because you're psychic — because you've seen the pattern.

That's most of what AI is doing.

## Body

A modern AI system has been "fed" enormous amounts of stuff — text, images, sounds, clicks, choices — and trained to **find patterns** in it. When you give it something new, it uses the patterns it learned to make a guess.

That's it. That's the trick.

When you type "thank you for the" into a text message and your phone suggests "gift" or "ride" — that's a pattern AI learned from looking at millions of texts.

When a face-unlock works on your phone — it's a pattern (your face) it was trained to recognize.

When ChatGPT writes a paragraph that sounds smart — it's predicting which words usually come after which other words, based on patterns it found in basically the whole internet.

## A definition that actually fits

**AI is a system that learns patterns from a huge amount of past examples, then uses those patterns to make guesses about new things.**

Three things matter in that sentence:

- **Learns patterns** — it doesn't think the way you think. It finds shapes in data.
- **Past examples** — every guess it makes is based on stuff that already happened. If something hasn't happened yet, AI is bad at it.
- **Guesses** — it can be confidently wrong. We'll spend three pages on that later.

## Sidebar

> A friend who has eaten lunch with you for a year is using "AI" the same way modern AI does — looking at what happened before and using it to predict what's next. The difference is the AI saw a million friends, not one.
