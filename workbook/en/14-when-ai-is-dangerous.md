# Spread 14 — When AI is dangerous: deepfakes, scams, manipulation

> Part 4: When it's wrong · spread 14 of 16

## Heading

**When AI is dangerous.**

## Body

AI being wrong is a problem. AI being **weaponized** is a different problem.

This page is about the things AI is being used to do that you should know about — not because they'll happen to you tomorrow, but because the day they do, you'll already know.

## 1. Deepfakes — fake video and audio that look real

AI can now generate video that shows real people doing or saying things they never did. The technology is good enough that the videos can fool casual viewers — and getting better fast.

What this means:

- A video of a politician saying something outrageous **might not be real**.
- A video of a classmate doing something embarrassing **might not be real**.
- A video of *you* doing something **might not be real**, even if everyone you know thinks it is.

### How to handle it

- Before believing or sharing a shocking video, ask: **who benefits from me believing this is real?**
- Look for the original source. If you can't find a credible news outlet covering it, that's a flag.
- Reverse-image search a frame. (Free at images.google.com or tineye.com.)
- Most importantly: **slow down**. Outrage is a deepfake's best weapon.

## 2. Voice cloning — fake calls that sound like family

AI can now copy a person's voice from a few seconds of audio. There are scams happening right now where someone calls a grandparent saying "Grandma, I'm in trouble, please send money" — using a voice cloned from a public video.

This is real. It's happening. It's been used against families in your county.

### How to handle it

- **Agree on a family code word.** Pick a word everyone knows. If a call sounds like family but feels off, ask for the code word. Real family will know it. A scammer won't.
- If a call from "family" asks for money urgently — hang up, then call back the person directly using the number you already have saved.
- Tell your parents, your grandparents, your aunts and uncles about this. Older family members are the primary targets.

## 3. Manipulation at scale — content designed to get you angry

AI now generates so much content (text, video, images, "news" articles) that it's increasingly hard to tell what was made by people and what was made to manipulate people.

A lot of it is designed to make you **angry**. Anger drives clicks, shares, and engagement — which is what advertisers pay for.

### How to handle it

- If something you saw made you instantly furious, **wait an hour before sharing it**.
- Ask: *what would I have to verify before I'd be willing to bet I'm right?*
- Be more skeptical of content that feels designed to confirm what you already believed. AI is very good at making that.

## The big idea

> Most AI is just useful. Some AI is being used to harm people — particularly through deception. **The protection isn't more technology. The protection is the same as it's always been: slow down, verify, talk to your family.**
