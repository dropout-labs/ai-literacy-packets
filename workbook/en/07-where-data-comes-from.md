# Spread 07 — Where data comes from (and what's left out)

> Part 2: How does it work? · spread 7 of 16

## Heading

**Where data comes from — and what's left out.**

## Opening question

> If you trained an AI on every photo ever posted to Instagram, what kinds of people, places, and lives would it think were "normal"?

Think for a minute before reading on.

## Body

Most of the data the world's biggest AI systems were trained on came from one place: **the internet**.

That sounds neutral. It isn't.

The internet — and the photos and writing on it — over-represents:

- People who already have phones and reliable internet
- Cities, especially cities in wealthy countries
- English (and a handful of other languages)
- The lives of people with the time and devices to post

It under-represents:

- People without smartphones, or with shared family devices
- Rural communities, agricultural communities
- Languages other than English (Spanish included — nowhere near its real share of the world)
- The lives of older relatives, working parents, people whose days don't fit on social media

When you ask a popular AI tool a question and it gives you an answer, the answer is shaped by **what was in the data**. If the data did not include your community, the AI will know less about your community. If the data did not include your language at full volume, the AI will speak your language worse.

This is not a glitch. This is **how the technology works**.

## A real example

A few years ago, soap dispensers with motion sensors were built and tested mostly on people with light skin. When dark-skinned people tried to use them, many didn't trigger.

The technology wasn't lying. It was working exactly as designed — on the people whose hands it had been built around.

AI works the same way, at much bigger scale.

## The big idea

> What's in the data shapes what AI knows. **What's missing from the data shapes what AI gets wrong.**

## Sidebar — your community

Think about your community for a moment.

- Does it show up online the way it actually is?
- What about your community would an AI never learn from looking at the internet?
- What would you want an AI to know that it probably doesn't?
