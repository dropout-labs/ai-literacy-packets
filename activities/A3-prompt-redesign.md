# A3 — Prompt Redesign

> **Pairs with spread 10** · ~15 minutes · pencil only

## What this activity teaches

How to ask AI for what you actually want. Why specificity matters. How to recognize a vague prompt and rewrite it into a clear one — without ever needing to actually run the prompt through an AI.

## What's on the page

Three "before" prompts, each with empty space below for the youth to write a "rewritten" version.

### Bad Prompt 1

> "tell me about dogs"

**Rewrite it so an AI would give you something useful:**

(blank space, ~6 lines)

### Bad Prompt 2

> "help with homework"

**Rewrite it so an AI would give you something useful:**

(blank space, ~6 lines)

### Bad Prompt 3

> "write something about my town"

**Rewrite it so an AI would give you something useful:**

(blank space, ~6 lines)

## Instructions (printed on the spread)

> A good prompt tells the AI three things:
>
> 1. **What you want** (a list? a poem? an explanation? a step-by-step?)
> 2. **What you already know** (so it doesn't waste time on basics)
> 3. **Who it's for** (you? a 6-year-old? a teacher?)
>
> Read each bad prompt below. Rewrite it to include those three things.

## Worked example (in a sidebar, not blank)

> **Bad:** "tell me about cars"
>
> **Better:** "Make me a list of 5 questions to ask a mechanic when I'm buying a used car for under $5,000. I don't know much about engines. The list is for me — I'm 16."

## Reflection prompt at the bottom

> Look at your three rewrites. Which one was hardest? Why?
>
> Which is more powerful: a person who knows the answer, or a person who knows how to ask the right question?

## Expected reasoning

The youth practices specificity in a low-stakes paper context. They produce three concrete examples of "before/after" prompt design that they can apply later when they actually use AI tools.

The reflection prompt at the bottom plants the seed that **prompt design is a skill, and it is the skill that determines whether AI is useful to you or not**.

## Facilitator notes

- It's fine if the rewrite is shorter than the worked example. Better short and specific than long and vague.
- Some youth will write rewrites that include private info ("write a poem about my grandma who died") — affirm that this is fine on paper but worth thinking about before pasting into a real AI tool.
